Binakol

Ingredients

2 stalks Lemongrass
1 tbsp Canola oil
1 Onion, peeled and sliced thinly
3 cloves Garlic, peeled and minced
1 thumb-size Ginger, peeled and julienned
1 (about 3 to 4 pounds) Whole native chicken, cut into serving parts
1 tbsp Fish sauce
4 cups Coconut juice
1 cup Young coconut meat (buko)
1 Green papaya, peeled and cut into cubes
1 bunch Spinach, stemmed and leaves separated

Instructions

STEP 1

Cut the stem and leaf ends of the lemongrass. Peel off a few layers to reach the white part. Cut into 2-inch lengths and pound with the back of a knife to release flavor. Set aside.

STEP 2

Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add onions, garlic, and ginger. Cook until softened.

STEP 3

Add chicken and cook until its color changes and juices run clear.

STEP 4

Stir in fish sauce and cook for about 1 minute.

STEP 5

Pour in coconut juice and bring to a boil, skimming off any scum that may float on top.

STEP 6

Add the pounded lemongrass.

STEP 7

Lower the heat, cover, and continue cooking for about 20 to 25 minutes or until the chicken is thoroughly cooked.

STEP 8

Introduce papaya and cook for about 3 to 5 minutes or until it becomes tender.

STEP 9

Add coconut meat and cook until heated through.

STEP 10

Season with salt and pepper to taste.

STEP 11

Add spinach leaves, pushing them down into the broth. Turn off the heat, cover the pot, and serve the dish hot.
